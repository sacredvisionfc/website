01 — About the Club

MORE THAN A FOOTBALL CAMP

Sacred Vision Football Academy is a GFA-registered club competing in the Central Region Division 2 League. Based in Enyan-Abaasa, Ghana's Central Region, we develop players through competitive football and professional coaching. Our summer camp opens the academy to local and international players — elite training and the authentic Ghana experience no camp in Europe or America can offer. The club is supported by Sacred Vision Foundation, a non-profit dedicated to youth development through sport.

⚽
6 Days Per Week Training
Intensive coaching six days a week. Technical drills, tactical sessions, and competitive small-sided games every day.
🏋️
Gym & Strength Training
Dedicated gym sessions to build power, speed, and athletic performance. Structured programmes tailored to each player's development.
🏊
Swimming & Recovery
Pool-based recovery sessions and swimming to aid conditioning, injury prevention, and active rest between training days.
🎥
Video & Tactical Analysis
Afternoon sessions breaking down footage and positional play — the same methods used by professional academies.

Professional Mentorship
One-on-one mentorship sessions with professional players. Guidance on career pathways, mindset, and what it takes to go pro.
🏆
Friendly Matches & Talent Evaluation
Competitive friendly matches against local teams with formal talent evaluation. Scouts and coaches assess players for future opportunities.
🍽️
Full Board Meals
Three Ghanaian & Continental meals daily. Breakfast: oatmeal, eggs, sausage, tea. Lunch: jollof rice, fried rice, palava sauce. Dinner: banku with tilapia, yam, rice ball with groundnut soup and more.

Standard rooms with air conditioning, TV, study table, and private washroom. Comfortable, secure, and close to training facilities.

07 — FAQs

YOUR QUESTIONS ANSWERED

Everything families need to know. Still have questions? Contact us on WhatsApp — we reply within 24 hours.

What ages is the camp open to? +
Sacred Vision Football Academy Summer Camp is open to players aged 15 to 25. All skill levels welcome — from beginners to competitive academy players.
Can international players attend? Do I need a visa? +
Yes — open to players from anywhere in the world. Most nationalities require a visa to enter Ghana. We provide a formal invitation letter. Full payment is required before the letter is issued.
How does rolling enrolment work? +
Choose any Monday start date between June 15 and August 30. Pick your programme: 2 weeks, 1 month, or 3 months. Pay a 50% deposit and your place is confirmed. Training runs 6 days per week.
Is accommodation included? +
Available for out-of-region and international campers. Participants are housed in standard rooms equipped with air conditioning, TV, study table, and a private washroom. Room rates range from GHS 250 to GHS 280 per day. Confirm your needs at registration.
What should campers bring? +
Football boots, shin pads, water bottle, sunscreen, and personal clothing. Camp kit is provided. A full packing list is sent after registration.
Do sessions run in English? +
Yes. All coaching sessions are conducted in English. International players will feel completely comfortable throughout.
What is the refund policy? +
The 50% deposit is non-refundable. The remaining 50% is refundable if cancelled more than 21 days before your start date.

🇬🇭 Explore Ghana

YOUR WEEKEND ADVENTURES

The Central Region is home to Ghana's most iconic landmarks. Weekend excursions take you beyond the pitch to experience history, nature, and culture — for a well-rounded experience combining historic castles with rainforest adventure.

Cape Coast Castle
A major historical site with dungeons and the "Door of No Return," detailing the history of the transatlantic slave trade. UNESCO World Heritage site, just 30 minutes from camp.
Kakum National Park
Known for its rainforest canopy walkway, offering a thrilling high-altitude view of nature 40 metres above the forest floor. One of only a handful in Africa.
Elmina Castle
The first European-built structure in sub-Saharan Africa, offering deep historical tours. A must-visit for every international camper.
Assin Manso Slave River
A significant heritage site where enslaved Africans had their last bath before being taken to the coastal castles. A deeply moving and educational experience.
Beaches & Fishing Villages
The Central Region is dotted with palm-fringed beaches and traditional fishing villages. Relax, explore, and experience authentic coastal Ghanaian life.
